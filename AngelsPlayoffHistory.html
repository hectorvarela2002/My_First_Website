The Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim

Established in 1961

The Angels have made 10 playoff appearances since 1961. Their first postseason appearance was in 1982. This team was led by Reggie Jackson and Rob Carew who led the Angels to the American League Championship Series (ALCS). The Angels would make it back to the ALCS in 1986, 2002, 2005 and 2009. In 2002 the Angels won the World Series beating the San Francisco Giants in seven games. This was the Angels first World Series title in their history. The Angels made the playoffs five out of seven season from 2004 to 2010. This postseason run included the Angels winning three straight American League West titles and four in five seasons. In 2009, the Angels made the playoffs again and played the Red Sox in the American League Divisional Series (ALDS). The Angels made a big postseason run and swept the Red Sox 3 games to 0. The Angels made it back to the ALCS again in 2009, with their last appearance in 2005. The Angels last playoff appearance was in 2014 when they clinched the Wild Card but lost to the Kansas City Royals in the ALDS 3 games to 0.
