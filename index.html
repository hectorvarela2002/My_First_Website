The Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim

Established in 1961

The Los Angeles Angels are a professional Major League Baseball team. The Angels were established in 1961 and played their first season at Wrigley Field in South-Central Los Angeles. The following season the Angels moved to Chavez Ravine Stadium, which is now Dodger Stadium, and played there from, 1962 to 1965. The team moved again for the 1966 season to Anaheim Stadium. The Stadium was renamed to Edison International Field in 1997 but is now named Angel Stadium since 2003.

Since the establishment of the Angels organization, their success during the season and in the playoffs in remarkable. The Angels have made incredible postseason runs, one which ended in the Angels winning the World Series. Through these playoff appearances, key players helped guide the Angels to victory over their appearances. Some of these players are in the Angel Hall of Fame, have their number retired by the Angels, and are in the Major League Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown. In 2010, the MLB All-Star Game was held at Angel Stadium where the National League won by a score of 3 to 1.
